Overview

Tabbit is an all-in-one management app designed for small business owners. It streamlines day-to-day tasks that often feel overwhelming and mundane, reducing time spent on administration. Tabbit allows users to reclaim their creativity and focus, resulting in greater business efficiency and less stress.

Current Problem

Not enough creativity and too much logistics.

Being surrounded by creatives looking to sell their work  (including myself). By starting at markets and occasionally moving their business to a website, which is not as common. Due to how complicated it is to run an online business and a business in general. Compared to vending, where you have to mass-produce once every few months, and you sell it all at once.


The commitment to running a business can be daunting, on top of the numerous factors to consider:


  • whether the business is profitable

  • inventory management

  • business growth

  • lack of benefits (if gone full time)

  • book keeping

  • taxes

  • customer information

  • marketing and social media

  • shipping logistics

  • time management

  • price management

  • product creation (the extensive effort that goes into creative products)


That’s only to name a few factors.

Me selling my goods at an art market!

Problem Statement

How might we create a simplistic but intuitive business manager for creatives? That helps artists understand the logistical side of their business.

Goals

1.

Simplify business tasks for creatives using the Law of Prägnanz.

2.

Design an all-in-one tool tailored to solo creatives so they feel supported, not overlooked.

3.

Allow creatives to focus on their craft!

Research Phase

Background

I interviewed 5 young, creative small business owners.

Asking the right questions:

  • • Walk me through a typical day or week in your creative business.

  • • What tools or software do you currently use to manage your business operations?

  • • What are the biggest challenges you face in managing your business?

  • • Are there things you wish you could do more efficiently or professionally?

  • • If you could design your dream app or assistant to help with your creative business, what would it do?

What did I find?

  • • 100% struggled with doing their finances properly and on time.

  • • 100% 100% want an easy finance tool (receipts, expenses, taxes).

  • • 80% Rely on family for tax help (mom/brother).

  • • 60% want better better order management/organization.

  • • 80% Rely on keeping their physical receipts in a box.

  • • 100% work on the business alone.

User Research

Meet our business owners.

Based on tailoring demographics, user interviews, and online. I created one user persona to empathize with and focus on.  I noticed the trend of lacking time and information among University students, whether that be through interviews or videos I watched of small business creators.

❮ ❯

What's Betty's business life like?

With the research gathered, we looked at what Betty’s user journey through a product launch would be like, going through all the stresses she normally does.

Competitive Analysis

Looking through the tools used most by small business owners.

I analyzed 4 competitors, looking at their strengths and weaknesses. I found that most competitors were either too complex or too simple, lacking the balance needed for solo creatives. Many also had poor user interfaces, making them difficult to navigate.

Information Architecture

Structuring the app for optimal user experience.

Finding what features were crucial was the most important and difficult part because of the number of factors that go into a business.

I needed to pick what features were key to the project because of the time constraints of needing to finish this project before school.

Wireframes

Sketching out the app's layout and functionality.

I developed low-fidelity wireframes to map out the app's interface and user interactions. This allowed for quick iterations and feedback, ensuring that the design met user needs before moving on to high-fidelity designs.

Design System

Friendly, welcoming and inclusive.

The main goal of the design system was to create a welcoming brand identity. Accessible to all.
