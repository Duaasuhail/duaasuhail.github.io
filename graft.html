Overview

Graft is an mobile app that targets Millennials by gamifying gardening and encouraging community. Providing an accessible and friendly guide with forums and achievements.

Short on Time?

Current Problem

Loneliness and the lost art of hobbies.

Hobbies are being reframed as another form of productivity or monetization rather than pure enjoyment. How there is a guilt around spending time doing something for their enjoyment rather than trying to make money constantly. When hobbies are crucial to maintaining healthy mental health. Which helps create space for creativity, self expression and relaxation. Combatting loneliness by providing conversation topics and social connection between humans.

Problem Statement

How might we design a platform for millennials to build new hobbies while fostering a community and connections.

Goals

1.

Help millennials find a sense of community.

2.

Push for millennials to indulge in hobbies.

Research Phase

Background

I interviewed 4 millennial gardeners ranging from beginner to extremely experienced.

Asking the right questions:

  • • Do you think you have a green thumb?

  • •What are the major issues you face when gardening?

  • • How attached are you to your plants?

  • • Where do you find information to care for your plants?

What did I find?

  • • 50% encountered their plants dying often (no green thumb still a newbie)

  • • 75% also have friends who garden.

  • • 100% would love to show off their plants progress.

  • • 75% of interviewees were interested in gamification and a trophy system.

User Research

Meet our business owners.

Based on tailoring demographics, user interviews, and online. I created one user persona to empathize with and focus on. I noticed the trend of lacking time and information among University students, whether that be through interviews or videos I watched of small business creators.

Information Architecture

Structuring the app for optimal user experience.

Finding what features were crucial was the most important and difficult part because of the number of factors that go into a business.

I needed to pick what features were key to the project because of the time constraints of needing to finish this project before school.

Wireframes

Sketching out the app's layout and functionality.

I developed low-fidelity wireframes to map out the app's interface and user interactions. This allowed for quick iterations and feedback, ensuring that the design met user needs before moving on to high-fidelity designs.

Design System

Friendly, welcoming and inclusive.

The main goal of the design system was to create a welcoming brand identity. Accessible to all.

Conclusion

What I Learned

Less is More.

Being able to apply the Law of Prägnanz and ensure I had all the necessary information was both time-consuming and very rewarding. Being able to capture everything important while keeping the design clear and intuitive.

Complicated Problems can have simple solutions.

The complexity of an issue doesn’t intimidate me. Regardless of how complicated business management can be, there is still a simple and efficient solution available. You just have to think and put in the time and effort to research, ideate, and create.

Research, Research, and more Research.

The amount of hours that were put into researching businesses and how to manage on, as well as who was managing businesses. Even after the initial research stage, the research never stopped. Especially when it came to learning about how taxes work and how expenses play that part. Revealing more pain points and complications when it came to business management.

Next Steps

Experiment with AI to create a mini assistant.

My research showcased that typically, creatives are against AI usage, so for this project, I avoided incorporating it. But creating a sister app with integrated AI to speed up the process and solve problems I didn’t this time around (the social media burnout issues). Creating simple schedules and timetables for social media posting.

Expand to desktop.

Creating a separate web app that connects the mobile app to their laptops.

Create a collaborative platform.

Being able to expand and add features to create a collaborative app where multiple people run one business.
